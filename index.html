Everything about the Google Glass

by Gustavo Guanabara

Updated on April 23, 2020

Which is

O Google Glass is an eyewear accessory that enables users to interact with multiple content in augmented reality. Also called Project Glass, the electronic is capable of taking pictures from voice commands, sending instant messages and making video calls. Its launch is scheduled for 2014, and its price should be $ 1,500. Google Glass is currently in the testing phase and already has a video fully recorded with the device. In addition, the search company has registered new anti-theft patents and screen unlocking for the accessory.

Realease date

There is no specific and official date for the device to be released, yet. It may be available in demos from 2013, but its release for stores is at least 2014.

How it works

According to sources close to Google, the glasses will rely on a small LCD or AMOLED screen at the top and in front of the user's eyes. With the use of a camera and GPS, you can situate yourself, as well as select options with head movement.

What you can do with Google Glasses

Google's video shows that you can turn into a kind of "superhuman," since the device can tell you how many yards you are from your destination, whether the subway is open or closed, show the weather, agenda and even allow you to schedule meetings with only voice commands.

What's new in Glass

Google finally unveiled the complete specifications of Google Glass, and with it a surprise still unheard of in the market: the search giant will use an audio system based on driving transduction. Through the stems of the glasses, the sound will be transmitted to the ear of the user through microvibrations in certain bones of his head, without using any type of speaker.

In addition to the audio surprise, the screen mounted to the front of the user's eye also caught attention. There will be 640 x 360 pixels of resolution that, in proportion, would amount to a 25-inch high-definition monitor placed 2.5 meters away from the viewer.
